Zero profit conditions

$$ p_1 = c_1 (w,r) $$ $$ p_2 = c_2 (w,r) $$

Full employment conditions

$$ a_{1L}Y_1 + a_{2L}Y_2 = L $$ $$ a_{1K}Y_1 + a_{2K}Y_2 = K $$

Factors prices are insensitive. That is factor prices do not change with respect to change in the factor endowments.

Intuition behind the Factor price insensitivity

When labour endowment increases (capital constant), the output of the labour intensive sector expands and the output of the capital intensive industry will contract.

Therefore the factors in the capital intensive will be taken out of the production and will be employed in labour intensive industry.

Thus the capital labour intensity ratio \(a_{iL} \over a_{iK}\) in both industries remains constant.

Change in Endowments

To see this we will totally differentiate the full employment conditions:

$$ a_{1L}dY_1 + a_{2L}dY_2 = dL $$ $$ a_{1K}dY_1 + a_{2K}dY_2 = dK $$

Here we are keeping product prices constant. Therefore \(a_{iL}\) and \(a_{iK}\) are constant.

Jones Algebra

\(\hat{L}\) is weighted average of \(\lambda_{1L}\) and \(\lambda_{KL}\)

Expansion in the labour intensive sector is more than the expansion in labour endowment because the output of the other sector has contracted.
