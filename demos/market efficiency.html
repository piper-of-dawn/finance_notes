Market Efficiency

Market in which prices fully reflect the available information is called efficient.

Efficiency in Financial Markets

  • Prices are always forward looking.
    • Prices look at adjustment in response to three information subsets:
  • Weak form tests: Historical information.
  • Semi strong form tests: Other information which is publically available.
  • Strong form tests: Do some investors have a monopolistic access to information relevant to price information.

What do we mean by fully reflect?

Notationally it can be written as: $$ E(\tilde{p}_{j,t+1}|\Phi_t) = [1+E(\tilde{r}_{j,t+1}|\Phi_t)]p_{j,t} $$

  • \(p_{j,t}\) is the price of the security \(j\) at time \(t\)
  • \(p_{j,t+1}\)is the price of the security \(j\) at time \(t+1\).
  • \(\Phi_t\) is the information available at time \(t\).
  • \(\tilde{r}_{j,t+1}\) = \({p_{j,t+1} - p_{j,t}} \over p_{j,t}\)
  • \(~\) superscript denotes a random variable.

There is some theory that relates the available information under uncertainity to the expected return at the time \(t+1\) i.e \(E(\tilde{r}_{j,t+1}|\Phi_t)\).

The expectations are formed on the basis of \(\Phi_t\) have a major empirical implication.
