Consumption based CAPM or CCPAM

The consumption capital asset pricing model (CCAPM) is an extension of the capital asset pricing model (CAPM) that uses a consumption beta instead of a market beta to explain expected return premiums over the risk-free rate. While the CAPM formula relies on the market portfolio's return to predict future asset prices, the CCAPM relies on aggregate consumption.

    Empirics of CAPM

    CAPM is based on many restrictive assumptions that do not hold true in real world. Hence it needs to be tested against the real data.

    Hence the test for the CAPM model is based on the following three assumptions:

    1. The expected returns on all assets are linearly related to their bettas and no other variable as marginal explanatory power. This is what the model obviously implies. The CAPM theory does not allow any other explanatory variable except \(beta\).
    2. Beta premium is positive.

    Most of the empirical tests of the CAPM model either use the cross section or the time-series regressions.

    Independent varibales are \(beta\)s. this means that error terms must contain no information. there is no correlation between any lags.
